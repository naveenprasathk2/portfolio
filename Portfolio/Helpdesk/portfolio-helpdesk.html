01 — Project overview

Digitising facility management for property tenants

Smart Helpdesk is a multi-tenant facility management app built for Nanosoft's ERP platform. It allows property tenants to submit maintenance complaints, track service requests, manage unit details, and communicate with facility management teams — all from their mobile device.

Users
Property tenants, facility managers, maintenance technicians

02 — Research & discovery

Understanding the pain points

Through stakeholder interviews and analysis of the existing workflow, I identified critical friction points in how tenants report issues and track maintenance progress.

The problem

  • Tenants relied on phone calls and emails to report maintenance issues — no centralised system
  • No visibility into request status once submitted; tenants couldn't track progress
  • Manual paper-based complaint forms led to frequent data entry errors and lost requests
  • Property managers struggled with disconnected asset, contract, and billing information

The solution

  • A structured multi-step complaint submission flow that guides tenants through each field
  • Real-time request tracking with status updates, ETA dates, and technician assignment
  • Rich media capture — photos, audio recordings, video evidence — attached directly to requests
  • Integrated modules for units, assets, contracts, and billing in a single cohesive experience

06 — Interaction design deep-dive

The complaint submission flow

The centrepiece of the app — a 3-step guided wizard that transforms a complex, error-prone process into a structured, intuitive experience.

1

Step indicator

Visual progress through Property → Complaint → Details. Reduces cognitive load by showing where the user is and how much remains.

2

Multi-modal evidence capture

Photo capture, audio recording, and video recording — three distinct channels for tenants to document issues. This reduces the need for technicians to revisit for assessment.

3

Scheduling with guard rails

Date and time picker with a validation note: "Your from time should be after 1 hour from now if preferred date is today." Prevents unrealistic scheduling and reduces back-and-forth.

4

Digital signature

Signature pad provides a digital confirmation of the complaint submission, adding accountability and a clear audit trail for facility management.

5

Progressive disclosure

By splitting across 3 steps, the form never overwhelms. Each step has a focused purpose: select property, describe complaint, add supporting details.

1

Step 1: Property selection

The first step collects property-specific information through structured input fields. Each field is clearly labeled with a floating label pattern for context.

2

Consistent navigation

Back and Next buttons remain fixed at the bottom, providing a predictable interaction pattern. Users always know how to progress or retreat.

3

Error prevention over error correction

Required fields are clearly marked with asterisks. The teal underline indicates the active input state, providing immediate visual feedback during data entry.

What I learned

Designing for enterprise users taught me that simplicity in complex domains is about progressive disclosure — showing the right information at the right time, not hiding complexity entirely. The multi-step wizard pattern was the single most impactful decision in reducing errors.

What I'd do differently

With more time, I would have conducted formal usability testing sessions with real tenants before handoff. I'd also explore offline-first capabilities for areas with poor network connectivity, which is common in large facilities.

Human factors thinking

This project strengthened my approach to error prevention over error correction — a principle I carry into every design. The structured inputs, validation guard rails, and digital signature all serve to reduce use errors before they happen.

Cross-functional collaboration

Working closely with backend developers on the ERP integration taught me how data architecture shapes the user experience. Understanding the technical constraints early allowed me to design feasible solutions from day one.
